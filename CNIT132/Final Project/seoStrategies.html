Explanation of Keywords Search

I used Wordtacker(free tool) to choose these keywords.First, I searched for "CCSF" because I took my CNIT 132 class in CCSF. The search volume of "CCSF" is around 22200, and the competition is high. So I changed "CCSF" to "CCSF Classes" which has approximately 400 search volume, and the rank competition is not as high as "CCSF." Then, I tried to search for keywords "Assignment", "Report", "Project", and "Profile". These keywords all have large searches and high competition. So I added one or two words to these keywords to lower the competition and make the keywords more specific. Moreover, I searched for "computer science" and "front end web developer". Both keywords have high competition and search volume, but I still want to use these because these will be the section headers in sections of my websites.

Tips for Searching Keywords
  • The higher the search volume for a given keyword or keyword phrase, the more work is typically required to achieve higher rankings.
  • The higher the search volume, the greater the competition and effort required to achieve organic ranking success
  • Long tail keywords with lower search volume often convert better, because searchers are more specific and intentional in their searches.
  • It could be a good idea to prioritize high-volume keywords that your competitors are not currently ranking for.
  • Knowing about seasonal trends can be advantageous in setting a content strategy.
  • There are five major categories of intent: informational queries, navigational queries, transaction queries, commercial investigation, and local queries. Depending on these different queries, we can design our keywords to fit seachers' interest.
